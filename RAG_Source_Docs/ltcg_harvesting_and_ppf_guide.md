# LTCG Harvesting and PPF: Two Powerful Tax‑Free Wealth Tools

Two of the most underutilised tax benefits in India are **LTCG harvesting** (making stock profits tax‑free) and **PPF** (the only true “EEE” investment). This guide explains both in detail.

## Part 1: LTCG Harvesting – The Legal Tax Hack

### What is LTCG?

Long‑Term Capital Gains (LTCG) are profits from selling assets held for more than:
- **1 year** for listed stocks and equity mutual funds
- **3 years** for debt mutual funds, gold, and real estate

For listed stocks/equity funds, LTCG up to **₹1,25,000 per financial year** is completely **tax‑free**. Gains above ₹1,25,000 are taxed at 10% (without indexation) or 20% with indexation for other assets.

### The Harvesting Hack

Because the exemption is **per year** and resets every April 1, you can “harvest” gains each year to stay within the tax‑free limit.

**Step‑by‑step:**
1. Identify stocks or mutual funds that have an **unrealised gain** (current price > purchase price).
2. Sell enough units so that the profit equals exactly ₹1,25,000 (or less).
3. Immediately buy back the **same number of units** (or similar) at the current market price.
4. Your capital gain of ₹1,25,000 is realised and tax‑free.
5. Your new purchase price is now higher – meaning future gains will be smaller, reducing future tax.

**Example:**  
You bought 1,000 shares of XYZ Ltd at ₹100 each (cost ₹1,00,000). Current price is ₹300. Unrealised gain = ₹2,00,000.  
You sell 625 shares:  
Sale proceeds = 625 × ₹300 = ₹1,87,500.  
Cost of those shares = 625 × ₹100 = ₹62,500.  
Gain = ₹1,25,000 – tax‑free.  
You then buy 625 shares again at ₹300 – new cost basis = ₹1,87,500. You still own the same number of shares, but you have saved ₹12,500 in tax (10% of ₹1,25,000) that you would have paid otherwise.

### Important Rules

- **Wash sale?** India has no “wash sale” rule for equities. You can buy back immediately.
- **Brokerage and taxes:** Factor in brokerage fees and securities transaction tax (STT) – they are usually small for a ₹1.25 lakh transaction.
- **Applicable only for gains:** You cannot harvest losses to reduce tax – that’s tax‑loss harvesting (a different strategy). Here we harvest gains to use the exemption.

## Part 2: PPF – The “God Tier” Tax‑Free Investment

Public Provident Fund (PPF) is a 15‑year government‑backed savings scheme with the unique **EEE** status:

- **E**xempt on investment (deduction under Section 80C – only in Old Regime)
- **E**xempt on interest earned (every year, tax‑free)
- **E**xempt on withdrawal at maturity (no tax on the final corpus)

Even if you are in the **New Regime** (where 80C deduction is not available), PPF still offers **tax‑free interest** and **tax‑free maturity** – which is better than any fixed deposit where interest is taxable.

### Current Features (as of FY 2025-26)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Interest rate | ~7‑8% per annum (compounded yearly, reset quarterly) |
| Minimum deposit | ₹500 per year |
| Maximum deposit | ₹1,50,000 per year |
| Lock‑in | 15 years (extendable in blocks of 5 years) |
| Partial withdrawal | Allowed from the 7th financial year |
| Loan against PPF | Available from 3rd to 6th year |

### Why PPF Beats Fixed Deposits

A bank FD giving 7.5% interest for someone in the 30% tax bracket yields an **effective post‑tax return** of only ~5.25% (because interest is added to income and taxed). PPF’s 7‑8% is **completely tax‑free** – equivalent to a taxable FD giving ~11‑12% interest.

### Strategy for Beginners

- **Start early:** Even ₹500 per month grows significantly due to compounding over 15 years.
- **Max it out if possible:** The ₹1.5 lakh annual limit is worth using if you have surplus funds.
- **Use as debt component:** In a portfolio, PPF acts as your low‑risk, tax‑efficient debt allocation.
- **Nominate:** Always register a nominee for your PPF account.

### Maturity and Extension

After 15 years, you can either:
- Withdraw the entire corpus (tax‑free).
- Extend for blocks of 5 years, continuing to earn the same interest, with one withdrawal allowed per year.

> **Combining LTCG harvesting (for equity) with PPF (for debt) gives you a near‑tax‑free investment portfolio – a powerful combination for long‑term wealth building.**
