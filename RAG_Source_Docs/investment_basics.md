# Investment Basics: Understanding Risk and Return

When investing your money, understanding the relationship between risk and return is fundamental. 

## The Core Concept
Generally, higher potential returns come with higher risk. 
- **Low Risk**: Savings accounts and government bonds are considered low risk. Your money is generally safe, but the returns will be lower, sometimes not even outpacing inflation.
- **High Risk**: Stocks and cryptocurrencies are high-risk investments. You stand a chance to gain significant wealth, but you could also lose your principal.

## Diversification
The best way to manage risk is through **diversification** — not putting all your eggs in one basket. By spreading your investments across multiple asset classes (bonds, real estate, domestic stocks, international stocks), you mitigate the risk of one bad performance wiping out your portfolio.

## Compounding
Albert Einstein allegedly called compound interest the "Eighth Wonder of the World." By starting early and reinvesting your earnings, your wealth can grow exponentially over time.
