# Gross Income and Total Deductions: Building Blocks of Taxable Income

Think of **Gross Total Income** as your “top line” – everything you earned. **Total Deductions** are the “tax shield” – amounts the government allows you to subtract before calculating tax.

## 1. Gross Income – The Five Heads of Income

Your total income is the sum of money you receive in a financial year **before** any tax is deducted. It is categorised into five heads:

- **Income from Salary:** Basic pay, HRA, bonuses, commissions, special allowances.
- **Income from House Property:** Rental income you receive from any house you own (after standard deduction of 30% for repairs).
- **Capital Gains:** Profits from selling stocks, mutual funds, or real estate (short‑term or long‑term).
- **Business / Professional Income:** Profits from freelancing, consulting, or running a business.
- **Income from Other Sources:** Interest from savings accounts, fixed deposits (FDs), dividends, lottery winnings, etc.

**Formula:**  
`Gross Total Income = Salary + Rent Received + Capital Gains + Interest/Dividends + Business Profits`

## 2. Standard Deduction – Automatic Reduction

- **New Regime:** ₹75,000 (salaried individuals and pensioners).
- **Old Regime:** ₹50,000.

You do not need any receipts – it is automatically allowed.

## 3. Section 80C – The Most Popular Deduction (Old Regime only)

**Limit:** ₹1,50,000 per financial year.  
**Eligible investments / expenses:**
- EPF / VPF (Employee Provident Fund / Voluntary Provident Fund)
- PPF (Public Provident Fund)
- ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme – tax‑saving mutual funds)
- LIC premiums (life insurance)
- Home loan **principal** repayment
- Tuition fees for up to two children (school/college)
- Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana
- National Savings Certificate (NSC)

## 4. Section 80D – Health Insurance (Old Regime only)

- **Self + family:** Up to ₹25,000
- **Parents (senior citizens, ≥60 years):** Additional ₹50,000
- Preventive health check‑ups are also covered within these limits.

## 5. Section 24(b) – Home Loan Interest (Old Regime only)

- **Limit:** ₹2,00,000 for a self‑occupied property.
- For a let‑out property, the entire interest is deductible (no upper limit).

## 6. HRA – House Rent Allowance Exemption (Old Regime only)

The exempt amount is the **least** of the following three:
- Actual HRA received
- Rent paid minus 10% of salary (Basic + DA)
- 50% of salary (for metro cities) or 40% (for non‑metros)

## Taxable Income Calculation

- **New Regime:**  
  `Taxable Income = Gross Income – ₹75,000 (Standard Deduction)`
- **Old Regime:**  
  `Taxable Income = Gross Income – (Standard Deduction + 80C + 80D + HRA + Section 24(b) + other exemptions)`

> **Note:** Most deductions (80C, 80D, HRA, 24b) are **only available under the Old Regime**. Under the New Regime, you only get the standard deduction and a few minor allowances (e.g., employer NPS contribution).
