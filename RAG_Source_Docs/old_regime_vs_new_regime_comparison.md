# Old vs. New Tax Regime: Detailed Comparison for FY 2026-27

The rules described here are effective from **April 1, 2026** (FY 2026-27 / AY 2027-28). The core trade‑off is: **Lower tax rates (New Regime) vs. Higher deductions (Old Regime).**

## Tax Slab Comparison

| Income Range (₹) | New Regime (Default) | Old Regime |
|-----------------|----------------------|------------|
| 0 – 2,50,000     | Nil                  | Nil        |
| 2,50,001 – 4,00,000 | Nil               | 5%         |
| 4,00,001 – 5,00,000 | 5%                | 5%         |
| 5,00,001 – 8,00,000 | 5%                | 20%        |
| 8,00,001 – 10,00,000 | 10%               | 20%        |
| 10,00,001 – 12,00,000 | 10%              | 30%        |
| 12,00,001 – 16,00,000 | 15%              | 30%        |
| 16,00,001 – 24,00,000 | 20% – 25%        | 30%        |
| Above 24,00,000    | 30%                  | 30%        |

## Section 87A Rebate – The “Hidden” Superpower

- **New Regime:** If taxable income ≤ ₹12,00,000 → tax = **zero** (effective zero‑tax up to ₹12,75,000 for salaried due to ₹75k standard deduction).
- **Old Regime:** If taxable income ≤ ₹5,00,000 → tax = **zero**.

## Deductions & Exemptions – What You Lose/Gain

| Feature                               | New Regime (FY 2026-27) | Old Regime               |
|---------------------------------------|-------------------------|--------------------------|
| Standard Deduction                    | ₹75,000                 | ₹50,000                  |
| Section 80C (PPF, LIC, ELSS, etc.)    | Not available           | Up to ₹1,50,000          |
| Section 80D (Health insurance)        | Not available           | Up to ₹75,000 (with parents) |
| Section 24(b) (Home loan interest)    | Not available (for self‑occupied) | Up to ₹2,00,000 |
| HRA (House Rent Allowance)            | Not available           | Exempt as per formula    |
| Child education allowance             | ₹3,000/month per child  | ₹100/month per child     |

> **What’s new in 2026?** The budget increased child education and hostel allowances under the New Regime to make it more attractive for families.

## Breakeven Logic – Which Regime Is Better?

The **breakeven point** is the amount of deductions (80C + 80D + HRA + home loan interest) you need to make the Old Regime cheaper than the New Regime.

- **General rule:** If your total deductions are **less than ₹3.75 – ₹4.25 lakh** (depending on income), the New Regime is almost always better.
- **Zero‑tax zone:** If your income is between ₹7 lakh and ₹12.75 lakh, the New Regime is strictly better because of the ₹60,000 rebate.

## Marginal Relief – The “Cliff Effect” Safety Net

If your income is just above ₹12 lakh (e.g., ₹12,05,000), you shouldn’t suddenly pay ₹60,000 in tax. **Marginal relief** ensures that the extra tax you pay is never more than the extra income above the threshold. This applies roughly between ₹12 lakh and ₹12.75 lakh.

## Regime Switch Rule

- **Salaried individuals:** Can switch between Old and New regimes **every year**.
- **Freelancers / Business owners:** Get **only one switch in their lifetime**. If you opt for Old Regime and later switch to New, you generally cannot go back to Old as long as you have business income. Choose wisely!
