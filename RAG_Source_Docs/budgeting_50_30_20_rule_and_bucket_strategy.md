# Budgeting 101: The 50/30/20 Rule and the Bucket Strategy

## The 50/30/20 Rule – A Simple Framework

This rule divides your **after‑tax income** into three categories:

- **50% for Needs (Survival)** – Essential expenses you cannot avoid.  
  Examples: Rent / EMI, groceries, electricity, internet, minimum debt payments, insurance premiums.
- **30% for Wants (Lifestyle)** – Discretionary spending that makes life enjoyable.  
  Examples: Dining out (Zomato/Swiggy), Netflix, weekend trips, new gadgets, hobbies.
- **20% for Savings & Debt Repayment (Future You)** – Money that builds wealth or reduces high‑interest debt.  
  Examples: Investments (mutual funds, PPF), emergency fund contributions, extra payments on credit cards or loans.

> **Why it works:** It’s simple. You don’t need to track every rupee – just ensure the proportions stay roughly correct. If “Needs” exceed 50%, you are living too large for your current income.

## Automating the 20% – “Pay Yourself First”

The most effective way to save 20% is to **automate** it. As soon as your salary hits your bank account, move that 20% to a separate investment or savings account – before you spend anything else. This removes the temptation to skip saving.

## The Two‑Account System (Lazy Tracking)

- **Account A (Bills & Investments):** Your salary is deposited here. Immediately transfer the 30% (Wants) to Account B. Also pay all bills, EMIs, and SIPs from this account.
- **Account B (Spend):** This is your “guilt‑free” card. Use it for dining, shopping, and entertainment. When it runs out, your fun for the month is over.

## Advanced Version – The 4‑Bucket Strategy

For better control, use four separate accounts:

| Account | Purpose | What goes in? |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| **Income Account** | Landing strip for all income | Salary, freelance payments, business receipts |
| **Survival Account** | Needs (50%) | Rent, utilities, groceries, minimum EMIs |
| **Fun Account** | Wants (30%) | Zomato, movies, shopping, hobbies |
| **Emergency Account** | Safety net (6 months of needs) | Never touch except for genuine emergencies – keep in a liquid fund or savings account |

## Using Technology to Track Expenses

Manual tracking is tedious. Use apps that automatically read your SMS or bank feeds:

- **Axio (formerly Walnut)** or **Moneyview** – Categorise spends from transaction SMS.
- **Neo‑banks (Jupiter, Fi Money)** – Provide built‑in spending insights and real‑time alerts.

## A Note on Credit Cards for Beginners

Credit cards offer rewards, but they are dangerous if misused. **Rule:** Treat your credit card like a debit card – never spend money you don’t already have in your bank. Always pay the **full bill** (not the minimum due) to avoid 40‑45% annual interest.
