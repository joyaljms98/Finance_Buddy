# Budgeting 101: The 50/30/20 Rule

The 50/30/20 rule is a simple and effective budgeting strategy designed to help people manage their expenses and build long-term wealth.

## How It Works
- **50% Needs**: Half of your after-tax income should go toward essential expenses like rent, utilities, groceries, and insurance.
- **30% Wants**: Thirty percent can be allocated to non-essentials like dining out, entertainment, and hobbies.
- **20% Savings & Debt Repayment**: The remaining twenty percent should be committed to savings, investments, and paying off debts beyond the minimum payments.

This rule is popular because of its simplicity. By categorizing expenses into just three buckets, it is easier to understand where your money is going and ensure that saving remains a priority.
