# Tax Planning Strategies for Indian Taxpayers (FY 2026-27)

Effective tax planning ensures you pay only what is legally required – not a rupee more. Here are the most powerful strategies based on Indian tax laws.

## 1. Presumptive Taxation – Section 44ADA (Freelancers & Professionals)

If you are a freelancer (developer, designer, consultant, doctor, etc.) earning up to **₹75 lakhs** and at least 95% of your receipts are through digital modes (bank/UPI), you can declare only **50% of your gross receipts** as taxable income. You don’t need to maintain detailed books or provide receipts for expenses.

**Example:**  
You earn ₹20 lakhs from freelance work. Under 44ADA, your taxable income = ₹10 lakhs. If you also use the New Regime (zero tax up to ₹12 lakhs), you pay **zero tax** on ₹20 lakhs of income!

## 2. Presumptive Taxation – Section 44AD (Small Businesses)

For small businesses (traders, shops, etc.) with turnover up to **₹3 crores** (and 95% digital receipts), you can declare only **6% of turnover** as taxable profit. This is an extremely low tax burden.

## 3. LTCG Harvesting – Make Your Stock Profits Tax‑Free

Long‑term capital gains (holding period >1 year for stocks/mutual funds) are taxed at 10% **only above ₹1.25 lakh** per financial year. Gains up to ₹1.25 lakh are completely tax‑free.

**The hack:** Every year (preferably in March), sell stocks or mutual funds that have made a profit – but only enough to realise a gain of exactly ₹1.25 lakh. Then immediately buy them back. You pay zero tax on that gain, and your new purchase price is higher (“cost reset”), reducing future taxes.

## 4. Use Tax‑Advantaged Accounts (Old Regime)

- **PPF** – Up to ₹1.5 lakh deduction under 80C, tax‑free interest, tax‑free maturity.
- **ELSS** – Equity‑linked savings scheme with 3‑year lock‑in; also under 80C.
- **NPS (National Pension System)** – Additional deduction of ₹50,000 under 80CCD(1B) over and above the 80C limit. Corporate NPS contributions (up to 10% of basic salary) are allowed even under the New Regime.

## 5. Health Insurance – Section 80D (Old Regime)

Premiums paid for health insurance for yourself, spouse, children, and parents are deductible:
- Up to ₹25,000 for self/family
- Up to ₹50,000 for senior citizen parents

This reduces taxable income while securing your health.

## 6. Advance Tax – Avoid Penalties

If your total tax liability (after TDS) is **more than ₹10,000**, you must pay advance tax in four installments:
- 15% by June 15
- 45% by September 15
- 75% by December 15
- 100% by March 15

Missing these deadlines attracts interest under sections 234B and 234C.

## 7. Charitable Donations – Section 80G

Donations to registered charities (e.g., Prime Minister’s Relief Fund, certain NGOs) qualify for a deduction of 50% or 100% of the donated amount, depending on the fund. Keep the receipt and the charity’s 80G registration number.

> **Always consult a tax professional for your specific situation. These strategies have different impacts depending on your income level, regime choice, and type of income.**
