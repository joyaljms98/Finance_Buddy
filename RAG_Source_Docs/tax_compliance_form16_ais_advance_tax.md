# Tax Compliance: Form 16, AIS, Advance Tax, and Filing Deadlines

Filing your income tax return (ITR) is not just about paying tax – it’s about **compliance**. Even if your tax is zero, filing an ITR is essential for visa applications, home loans, and carrying forward losses. Here’s what you need to know.

## Form 16 – Your Salary Tax Certificate

Form 16 is issued by your employer every year (typically by June 15) for the previous financial year. It contains:
- Your salary breakdown (basic, HRA, allowances, etc.)
- Deductions claimed by you (80C, 80D, etc. – under Old Regime)
- TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) deducted from your salary and deposited with the government

**Why it matters:** You need Form 16 to file your ITR. It acts as proof of your income and tax deducted.

## AIS – Annual Information Statement

AIS is a comprehensive statement available on the Income Tax e‑filing portal. It shows **all** financial transactions reported to the IT department by third parties:
- Interest earned on savings accounts and FDs (from banks)
- Dividends received (from companies/mutual funds)
- Sale/purchase of stocks and mutual funds (from brokers)
- Foreign remittances, credit card spends above a limit, etc.

**Crucial step:** Before filing your ITR, **download and verify your AIS**. If any transaction is incorrect (e.g., interest shown for an account you closed), you can submit a correction request online.

## Form 26AS – The Consolidated Tax Statement

Form 26AS is a part of AIS. It shows:
- TDS deducted by your employer, bank, or any other deductor
- TCS (Tax Collected at Source)
- Advance tax paid by you
- Self‑assessment tax paid

All taxes paid by you or on your behalf are reflected here. Ensure that the TDS shown in Form 16 matches Form 26AS.

## Deadlines for Filing ITR

For individuals whose accounts are not required to be audited (i.e., most salaried employees and freelancers not covered under presumptive taxation with turnover < ₹3 crore), the due date is **July 31** of the assessment year.

- For FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27): **July 31, 2026** (sometimes extended to August 31).
- Belated return: Can be filed until December 31 of the AY, but with a late fee of up to ₹5,000.

> **Always file on time** – a late return cannot be revised to claim a refund, and you lose the ability to carry forward certain losses.

## Advance Tax for Non‑Salary Income

If you have income from freelancing, business, capital gains, or interest that does not have TDS deducted, you must pay **advance tax** if your total tax liability after TDS is ₹10,000 or more.

**Due dates for advance tax (for individuals):**

| Installment | Due Date | Cumulative payment |
|-------------|----------|--------------------|
| 1st         | June 15  | 15% of total tax    |
| 2nd         | September 15 | 45% of total tax |
| 3rd         | December 15 | 75% of total tax |
| 4th         | March 15 | 100% of total tax   |

**Penalty for delay:** Interest under section 234B (for non‑payment) and 234C (for deferment) is charged at 1% per month.

## Filing Even When Tax Is Zero

Under the New Regime, many salaried individuals with income up to ₹12.75 lakh have **zero tax liability**. Still, you must file an ITR. Reasons:
- Visa applications (many embassies ask for last 2‑3 years’ ITRs)
- Home loan or car loan applications (banks want proof of income)
- Carry forward capital losses (losses can only be carried forward if you file a return before the deadline)
- Refund of excess TDS (e.g., if your bank deducted TDS on FD interest but your total income was below taxable limit)

## Step‑by‑Step Filing Summary

1. Collect Form 16 from employer.
2. Download AIS / Form 26AS from the income tax portal.
3. Verify all transactions – report discrepancies if any.
4. Calculate taxable income and tax payable (use online calculators or the portal’s pre‑filled form).
5. If any tax is due, pay it online (via challan ITNS 280).
6. File ITR (choose the correct form – ITR‑1 for salaried, ITR‑3 for freelancers/business).
7. E‑verify within 30 days (using Aadhaar OTP, net banking, or Digilocker).
