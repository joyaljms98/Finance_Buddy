# Presumptive Taxation: A Deep Dive for Freelancers & Small Business Owners

Presumptive taxation schemes (Sections 44ADA and 44AD) are the most powerful tax hacks in India for freelancers, consultants, and small business owners. They eliminate the need for tedious book‑keeping and allow you to declare profits at a very low percentage of your turnover.

## Section 44ADA – For Freelancers & Professionals

**Who qualifies?**  
Any individual (or partnership firm) carrying on a **profession** listed under section 44AA(1) – which includes lawyers, architects, accountants, doctors, engineers, interior decorators, and any other profession notified by the CBDT. **IT freelancers (developers, designers, digital marketers) also qualify** as “technical consultants.”

**Conditions:**
- Gross receipts ≤ ₹75 lakhs in the financial year.
- At least **95% of receipts** are through digital means (bank transfer, UPI, cheque, card) – cash is allowed only up to 5%.
- You are not already claiming a higher profit percentage.

**How it works:**  
You can declare **50% of your total gross receipts** as your taxable income. The remaining 50% is deemed to be your expenses – no receipts needed.

**Example:**  
You earn ₹40 lakhs from freelance work.  
Taxable income under 44ADA = ₹20 lakhs.  
You pay tax only on ₹20 lakhs – a huge reduction.

## Section 44AD – For Small Business Owners

**Who qualifies?**  
Any resident individual, HUF, or partnership firm (not LLP) carrying on any business except those specified (e.g., plying/hiring of goods carriages, agency business, or professions covered under 44ADA).  
Typical examples: retail shop, small manufacturing, trading, food stall, etc.

**Conditions:**
- Total turnover or gross receipts ≤ ₹3 crores.
- At least 95% of receipts are through digital modes.
- You do not claim any other deduction under sections 30 to 43 (like depreciation, rent, etc.) – the presumptive rate is all‑inclusive.

**How it works:**  
You declare **6% of your turnover** as taxable profit (or 8% if cash receipts exceed 5%). That’s it.

**Example:**  
Your shop has a turnover of ₹1 crore.  
Taxable income = ₹6 lakhs (6% of ₹1 crore).  
You pay tax only on ₹6 lakhs, even if your actual profit was much higher.

## GST Registration for Freelancers & Businesses

- **Threshold:** Once your turnover exceeds **₹20 lakhs** (₹10 lakhs in special category states), you **must** register for GST.
- **Export of services (international clients):** GST rate is **0%** (zero‑rated supply). However, registration is still mandatory, and you must file monthly/quarterly returns.
- **Input Tax Credit (ITC):** If you are registered, you can claim a refund of the GST paid on business purchases – laptop, internet bill, office furniture, etc. For a ₹1 lakh laptop, you can save roughly ₹18,000 in taxes.

## Important Rules & Caveats

- **No deduction for actual expenses:** Under presumptive taxation, you cannot claim separate deductions for rent, salaries, depreciation, etc. The deemed profit percentage (50% or 6%) is considered to cover all expenses.
- **Advance tax applies:** Even under presumptive taxation, you must pay advance tax if your liability exceeds ₹10,000.
- **One‑time regime switch:** If you use presumptive taxation under the Old Regime and later switch to the New Regime, you cannot go back to presumptive taxation under the Old Regime for that business – the switch is permanent.

## Which is better – Presumptive or Actual?

Use presumptive (44ADA/44AD) if:
- Your actual expenses are **less than** 50% of receipts (for freelancers) or less than 94% of turnover (for businesses) – because presumptive gives you a higher deemed profit than your actual profit.
- You want to avoid the cost and hassle of maintaining books and getting a tax audit.

Use actual accounting if:
- Your actual profit margin is **lower** than the presumptive rate (e.g., a high‑expense business with 3% actual profit – you would be overtaxed under 44AD’s 6%).
- You want to claim carried‑forward losses or depreciation on assets.

> **Always evaluate both methods before filing your return. For most freelancers with moderate expenses, 44ADA is a massive tax saver.**
