# Income Tax Basics: Financial Year, Assessment Year, and New Regime Slabs (FY 2025-26)

## What is a Financial Year (FY) and Assessment Year (AY)?

- **Financial Year (FY)** – The period in which you earn your income. In India, it runs from **April 1 to March 31** of the following year. For example, FY 2025-26 is from April 1, 2025 to March 31, 2026.
- **Assessment Year (AY)** – The year immediately after the FY, during which you file your income tax return (ITR) for the income earned in that FY. For income earned in FY 2025-26, the AY is 2026-27 (April 1, 2026 to March 31, 2027).

## Two Tax Regimes in India

The Indian tax system offers two ways to calculate your tax liability:

1. **Old Tax Regime** – Higher tax rates, but you can claim many deductions (e.g., 80C, 80D, HRA, home loan interest) to reduce your taxable income.
2. **New Tax Regime (Default)** – Lower tax rates, but you lose almost all deductions and exemptions. As of FY 2025-26, the **New Regime is the default** – if you don't actively choose the Old Regime, the New Regime will apply.

## New Tax Regime Slabs (FY 2025-26 / AY 2026-27)

These slabs apply to your taxable income **after** allowing only the standard deduction (₹75,000 for salaried individuals) and a few other specified deductions.

| Income Range (₹) | Tax Rate |
|-----------------|----------|
| 0 – 4,00,000    | Nil      |
| 4,00,001 – 8,00,000 | 5%   |
| 8,00,001 – 12,00,000 | 10%  |
| 12,00,001 – 16,00,000 | 15% |
| 16,00,001 – 20,00,000 | 20% |
| 20,00,001 – 24,00,000 | 25% |
| Above 24,00,000 | 30%      |

## Section 87A Rebate – The Zero‑Tax Benefit

If your **taxable income** (after the standard deduction) is **₹12,00,000 or less**, the government gives you a **rebate** that reduces your entire tax liability to **zero**. This is called the Section 87A rebate.

**For salaried individuals:** You also get a **Standard Deduction of ₹75,000**. That means you can earn up to **₹12,75,000** (₹12L + ₹75k) and still pay **zero tax** under the New Regime.

Example:  
- Gross salary = ₹12,50,000  
- Less: Standard Deduction = ₹75,000  
- Taxable income = ₹11,75,000 (which is ≤ ₹12L)  
- Tax as per slabs = some amount, but **rebate makes it zero**.

## Important Compliance Terms

- **Form 16** – A certificate from your employer showing how much tax (TDS) was deducted from your salary.
- **AIS (Annual Information Statement)** – A detailed statement that shows all financial transactions reported to the Income Tax Department (interest, dividends, stock sales, etc.). Always check this before filing your ITR.

## Filing Deadline

For individual taxpayers (non‑audit cases), the typical deadline is **July 31st** of the Assessment Year. For FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27), the due date is generally **July 31, 2026**, though sometimes it may be extended to August 31st.

> **Even if your tax is zero, you should file an ITR.** It is required for visa applications, home loans, and carrying forward losses.
