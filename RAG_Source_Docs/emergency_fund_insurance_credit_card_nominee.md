# The Safety Net: Emergency Fund, Insurance, Credit Card Wisdom, and Nominees

Before you think about investing in stocks or cryptocurrencies, you must build a **financial safety net**. In India, a single medical emergency or job loss can wipe out years of savings if you are not prepared.

## 1. Emergency Fund – 6 Months of “Needs”

Your emergency fund is money set aside exclusively for unexpected events: job loss, medical emergency, urgent home repair, etc.

**How much?**  
Calculate your monthly “Needs” (rent + groceries + EMIs + utilities + insurance premiums). Multiply by 6. That is your target.

**Where to keep it?**  
- A separate **high‑interest savings account** (e.g., small finance banks offering 7% interest)  
- A **liquid mutual fund** (redeemable within 24 hours)  
- A **fixed deposit** with instant withdrawal feature

**Rules:**  
- Never touch this fund for a vacation, a gadget, or a sale.  
- If you use it, replenish it as soon as possible.

## 2. Health Insurance – Don’t Rely Only on Your Employer

Your company’s group health insurance ends the day you resign, get laid off, or retire. You need **personal health insurance** that stays with you for life.

**Strategy:**  
- Buy a **base policy** of ₹5‑10 lakhs (for yourself and family).  
- Add a **super top‑up plan** of ₹25‑50 lakhs with a deductible equal to your base policy. This costs very little but covers catastrophic events.

**Senior citizen parents:**  
If your parents are aged 60+, premiums for their separate policy are eligible for an **additional ₹50,000 deduction** under Section 80D (Old Regime).

## 3. Term Insurance – If Someone Depends on Your Income

If you have a spouse, children, or elderly parents who rely on your earnings, you need a **term life insurance plan**. It is pure protection – no investment component – and very affordable.

**Example:**  
A healthy 30‑year‑old can get a **₹1 crore term cover** for an annual premium of around ₹10,000‑15,000.

**Rule:** Buy term insurance **online** directly from the insurer (not through agents selling expensive “endowment” plans). The cover should be 15‑20 times your annual income.

## 4. Credit Cards – The Reward Trap

Credit cards offer points, cashback, and lounge access – but they are designed to make you spend more. The interest rate on unpaid credit card bills in India is **40‑45% per year** – higher than almost any investment return.

**Three golden rules:**
- **Never pay “Minimum Due”** – always pay the full bill by the due date.
- **Utilisation ratio ≤ 30%** – If your limit is ₹1 lakh, never spend more than ₹30,000 in a month. This keeps your CIBIL score high.
- **Treat it like a debit card** – Only swipe if you already have that money in your bank account.

## 5. Nominee Registration – The Forgotten Step

Billions of rupees lie unclaimed in “frozen” bank accounts and investments because the account holder passed away without registering a nominee. **Every financial asset should have a nominee.**

Checklist:
- Bank savings and FDs → Nominee registered.
- Demat account (stocks, mutual funds) → Nominee added.
- PPF, EPF, NPS → Nominee updated.
- Insurance policies → Nominee clearly named (with percentage shares if multiple).

This small step ensures your loved ones can access the money without legal battles.

## The “Rainy Day” Checklist – Complete Before You Invest

- [ ] Emergency fund = 6 months of needs.
- [ ] Term insurance (if dependents exist) – ₹1 crore cover minimum.
- [ ] Personal health insurance (super top‑up) – not just company policy.
- [ ] Nominee registered on all assets.
- [ ] Credit card bill paid in full every month.
